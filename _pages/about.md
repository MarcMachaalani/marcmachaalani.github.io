Marc Machaalani, MD received his medical degree from the Lebanese University in 2023 and is currently a postdoctoral research fellow at the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute under the mentorship of [Dr. Toni K. Choueiri](https://labs.dana-farber.org/choueirilab/). His research focuses on integrative analyses of circulating and tumor biomarkers, with an emphasis on host immunity, gut dysbiosis, and tumor genomics as determinants of clinical outcomes in renal cell carcinoma.

He has authored and co-authored multiple high-impact publications in leading journals including Nature Medicine, Nature Communications, Journal of Clinical Oncology, JAMA Network Open, European Urology, and ESMO Open, with work spanning cancer metabolism, immune evasion, tumor microenvironment biology, circulating biomarkers, and health disparities. His research has been presented at major international meetings including ASCO, ASCO GU, ESMO, SITC, and the Kidney Cancer Research Summit through both oral and poster presentations. Dr. Machaalani is the recipient of several national and international honors, including multiple ASCO Conquer Cancer Merit Awards and ESMO Merit Awards, supported by competitive fellowships and travel grants.

Outside of medicine, he is an accomplished classical pianist and enjoys reading, biking, and playing tennis.
